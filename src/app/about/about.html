The JETS Lab is a collection of scholars committed to work that improves the environment and reduces environmental inequities. We tackle a set of problems that are particularly hard to solve from the grassroots level: disparities in access to meaningful information, cutting edge technology, and community-friendly scientific experts. We consider ourselves most successful when our academic research pays off for communities fighting for environmental justice.

JETS Lab Contributors

Gwen Ottinger received her Ph.D. from the Energy and Resources Group at the University of California, Berkeley, where she first began working with environmental justice activists and refinery communities on issues of air toxics monitoring. As an Assistant Professor in Drexel University’s Department of Politics and Center for Science, Technology, and Society (STS), she has amassed the usual list of publications and honors. She is especially grateful for the reception that her book, Refining Expertise: How Responsible Engineers Subvert Environmental Justice Challenges, has received from the STS community; it is a reminder of what can come from pouring heart and soul into a project. After completing the book, Ottinger packed three of her dream projects--a history of the air monitoring “buckets,” a participatory design project to put real-time air data to better use, and a community-based ethics course for aspiring scientists and engineers--into one NSF proposal, which was ultimately funded. She is delighted and humbled by the chance to carry out these projects in collaboration with exceptional students and collaborators.

If you are a student interested in working with the JETS Lab, please contact Professor Ottinger: ottinger@drexel.edu.
